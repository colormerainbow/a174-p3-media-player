Hi! I'm Kathy from Katalyst Fitness and Nutrition, and today I wanted to talk to you about protein and why it's so beneficial for weight loss.

So, when you're trying to lose weight, protein is the key to getting you to your goal. Research shows that most women may eat too little protein and the benefits of a higher daily protein intake include weight loss, increased metabolism, preserving your muscle as we age, and then maintaining a lean fat burning physique, which is what we're after.

So, proteins are made up of amino acids and these amino acids are needed for your muscles, for your blood, skin, hair, nails, and your internal organs. Your body produces some of these amino acids, but you must get the others known as the essential amino acids through your diet. So for the most part, eating animal protein like meat, fish, eggs, or dairy every day will provide all the essential amino acids that you need in the proper balance.

So to lose weight, you need to consume fewer calories than you burn, and eating protein can increase the number of calories that you burn by increasing your metabolic rate - that's your calories out and then reducing your appetite, which are your calories in.

So this is due to the thermic effect of food. It actually takes more energy to process and digest protein than it does to process carbs or fats, and that's going to lead to you burning as much as 80 to 100 calories a day just by eating protein.

So the biggest benefit of protein for weight loss is the satiety factor. So you're going to feel full longer and reduce your appetite, which again leads to a reduction in your overall calories. So eating more protein makes it way easier to stick to any weight loss diet.

And there was a study that showed women over 12 weeks who increased their protein intake to 30 percent of their total calories, ate 441 fewer calories per day and lost 11 pounds over that 12 weeks, just by eating more protein.

So, how much protein do you need? For weight loss, a protein intake at around 30 percent of your calories seems to be ideal, due to the increase in the metabolic rate that we discussed and the decrease in hunger and the reduction of overall calories.

So most studies suggest that 0.7 to 1 gram per pound of body weight is sufficient. The more active that you are, the more protein that you would need. So, if your job is physically demanding, or you do any sort of regular exercise, you're going to need to eat more protein.

So fueling your body properly with protein is going to get you your best results for weight loss while maintaining muscle and increasing your metabolism.
